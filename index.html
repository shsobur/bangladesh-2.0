Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Nobel Peace Prize winner, is a globally respected figure from Bangladesh. He founded Grameen Bank in 1983, pioneering the concept of microfinance and empowering millions of poor individuals, especially women, by providing small loans without collateral. His work has been credited with transforming lives and inspiring financial inclusion worldwide.


Despite his achievements, Dr. Yunus has faced challenges in Bangladesh. The government has accused him of financial irregularities, leading to legal cases and criticism. These actions sparked national and international debates about his treatment, with many seeing the allegations as politically motivated.
